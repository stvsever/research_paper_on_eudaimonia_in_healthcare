PHQ-9 | Patient Health Questionnaire-9 | A 9-item self-report measure of depressive symptom severity over the past two weeks, assessing frequency of anhedonia, depressed mood, sleep disturbance, fatigue, appetite changes, guilt, concentration difficulty, psychomotor changes, and suicidal ideation.
GAD-7 | Generalized Anxiety Disorder 7-item Scale | A 7-item self-report screening tool measuring the severity of generalized anxiety symptoms including worry, restlessness, irritability, and difficulty relaxing over the past two weeks.
BDI-II | Beck Depression Inventory-II | A 21-item self-report inventory measuring the severity of depressive symptoms including sadness, pessimism, loss of pleasure, guilty feelings, punishment feelings, self-dislike, crying, agitation, loss of interest, indecisiveness, worthlessness, loss of energy, sleep changes, appetite changes, concentration difficulty, fatigue, and loss of interest in sex.
BAI | Beck Anxiety Inventory | A 21-item self-report measure of anxiety symptom severity, focusing on somatic and cognitive symptoms of anxiety such as numbness, feeling hot, inability to relax, fear of the worst, heart pounding, unsteadiness, terrified, nervous, and feelings of choking.
DASS-21 | Depression Anxiety Stress Scales-21 | A 21-item self-report instrument measuring three related negative emotional states: depression (dysphoria, hopelessness, devaluation of life, self-deprecation, lack of interest, anhedonia, inertia), anxiety (autonomic arousal, skeletal muscle effects, situational anxiety, subjective experience of anxious affect), and stress (difficulty relaxing, nervous arousal, easily upset, irritable, over-reactive, impatient).
SCL-90-R | Symptom Checklist-90-Revised | A 90-item self-report symptom inventory measuring psychological distress across nine primary symptom dimensions: somatization, obsessive-compulsive, interpersonal sensitivity, depression, anxiety, hostility, phobic anxiety, paranoid ideation, and psychoticism.
MADRS | Montgomery-Asberg Depression Rating Scale | A 10-item clinician-rated scale measuring depression severity through apparent sadness, reported sadness, inner tension, reduced sleep, reduced appetite, concentration difficulties, lassitude, inability to feel, pessimistic thoughts, and suicidal thoughts.
HAM-D | Hamilton Depression Rating Scale | A clinician-administered 17-item scale assessing the severity of depressive symptoms including depressed mood, guilt, suicide, insomnia, work and activities impairment, retardation, agitation, anxiety, somatic symptoms, and loss of weight.
HAM-A | Hamilton Anxiety Rating Scale | A 14-item clinician-rated measure of anxiety severity evaluating anxious mood, tension, fears, insomnia, cognitive symptoms, depressed mood, somatic muscular and sensory symptoms, cardiovascular, respiratory, gastrointestinal, genitourinary, and autonomic symptoms.
STAI | State-Trait Anxiety Inventory | A 40-item self-report inventory measuring two distinct anxiety constructs: state anxiety (current feelings of apprehension, tension, nervousness, and worry) and trait anxiety (general tendency to perceive situations as threatening and respond with elevated anxiety).
PSQI | Pittsburgh Sleep Quality Index | A 19-item self-report measure assessing sleep quality and disturbances over a one-month period across seven component scores: subjective sleep quality, sleep latency, sleep duration, habitual sleep efficiency, sleep disturbances, use of sleeping medication, and daytime dysfunction.
PCL-5 | PTSD Checklist for DSM-5 | A 20-item self-report measure of post-traumatic stress disorder symptoms corresponding to the DSM-5 criteria: intrusion symptoms, avoidance, negative alterations in cognitions and mood, and marked alterations in arousal and reactivity following exposure to traumatic events.
AUDIT | Alcohol Use Disorders Identification Test | A 10-item screening instrument for hazardous and harmful alcohol consumption, measuring alcohol intake quantity and frequency, drinking behaviour and dependence symptoms, and adverse consequences of alcohol use.
CGI | Clinical Global Impression Scale | A clinician-rated 3-item scale providing a brief overall assessment of illness severity, global improvement or change over time, and therapeutic response incorporating both therapeutic efficacy and treatment-related adverse effects.
BSI | Brief Symptom Inventory | An 18-item self-report measure of psychological distress covering three dimensions: somatization (distress arising from perceptions of bodily dysfunction), depression (dysphoric mood and affect, lack of motivation, loss of interest in life), and anxiety (nervousness, tension, panic, apprehension, and somatic correlates of anxiety).
OQ-45 | Outcome Questionnaire-45 | A 45-item self-report measure designed to track patient progress in therapy across three domains: symptom distress (primarily anxiety and depression), interpersonal relations (loneliness, conflict, family and marriage difficulties), and social role performance (difficulties in employment, school, or household duties).
K10 | Kessler Psychological Distress Scale | A 10-item self-report questionnaire measuring non-specific psychological distress over the past 30 days, including feelings of nervousness, agitation, psychological fatigue, depression, hopelessness, restlessness, sadness, worthlessness, and effort required for daily activities.
CORE-OM | Clinical Outcomes in Routine Evaluation-Outcome Measure | A 34-item self-report measure of global psychological distress covering four domains: subjective well-being, problems and symptoms (anxiety, depression, physical complaints, trauma), life functioning (close relationships, general social functioning, daily functioning), and risk to self and others.
SDS | Sheehan Disability Scale | A 3-item self-report measure of functional impairment assessing the degree to which work or school, social life or leisure activities, and family life or home responsibilities are disrupted by symptomatology.
WHODAS-2.0 | WHO Disability Assessment Schedule 2.0 | A 36-item measure of health and disability assessing functioning across six domains: cognition (understanding and communicating), mobility (moving and getting around), self-care (hygiene, dressing, eating), getting along (interacting with others), life activities (domestic and work responsibilities), and participation (community involvement).
Y-BOCS | Yale-Brown Obsessive Compulsive Scale | A 10-item clinician-administered rating scale measuring obsessive-compulsive disorder severity by separately assessing the time occupied by, interference from, distress caused by, resistance against, and control over obsessions and compulsions.
PANSS | Positive and Negative Syndrome Scale | A 30-item clinician-rated instrument measuring symptom severity in schizophrenia across three subscales: positive symptoms (hallucinations, delusions, conceptual disorganization), negative symptoms (blunted affect, emotional withdrawal, poor rapport, passive social withdrawal), and general psychopathology.
EPDS | Edinburgh Postnatal Depression Scale | A 10-item self-report screening tool for postnatal depression measuring feelings of being overwhelmed, inability to cope, difficulty sleeping due to sadness, crying, self-harm thoughts, anxiety, and inability to enjoy things or find humour in life.
GAF | Global Assessment of Functioning | A clinician-rated numeric scale from 0 to 100 measuring overall psychological, social, and occupational functioning on a continuum from persistent danger of severe self-harm through superior functioning; used to track illness severity and treatment response.
C-SSRS | Columbia Suicide Severity Rating Scale | A clinician-administered scale measuring suicidal ideation and behavior through structured questions assessing severity and intensity of ideation (frequency, duration, controllability, deterrents, reasons), and categorisation of suicidal behaviour (actual attempts, interrupted attempts, aborted attempts, preparatory acts).
EQ-5D | EuroQol Five Dimensions | A standardised self-report measure of health-related quality of life assessing five dimensions: mobility, self-care, usual activities, pain and discomfort, and anxiety and depression, combined with a visual analogue scale for overall health state rating.
SF-36 | Short Form Health Survey-36 | A 36-item self-report survey measuring health-related quality of life across eight domains: physical functioning, role limitations due to physical health, bodily pain, general health perceptions, vitality, social functioning, role limitations due to emotional problems, and mental health.
MDQ | Mood Disorder Questionnaire | A self-report screening instrument for bipolar spectrum disorders assessing the lifetime presence of manic or hypomanic symptoms including elevated mood, irritability, increased confidence, decreased sleep need, pressured speech, racing thoughts, distractibility, increased energy, and risky behaviour.
YMRS | Young Mania Rating Scale | An 11-item clinician-administered scale measuring mania symptom severity including elevated mood, increased motor activity and energy, sexual interest, sleep disturbance, irritability, speech rate and amount, language-thought disorder, thought content, disruptive-aggressive behaviour, appearance, and insight.
PSS | Perceived Stress Scale | A 10-item self-report measure of the degree to which situations in one's life are appraised as stressful, assessing feelings of unpredictability, uncontrollability, and overload, and the frequency of being upset, feeling unable to cope, feeling nervous, feeling unable to control important things, and feeling difficulties are piling up.
